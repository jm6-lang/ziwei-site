# 疾厄宫

The **Health Palace** (疾厄宫, Ji Ai Gong) in Zi Wei Dou Shu is the palace that reveals your **health patterns, disease tendencies, and vulnerability to illness**. While not a medical diagnosis, this palace provides valuable insight into which body systems and life areas may require extra attention, enabling you to take preventive measures and live more consciously.

The Health Palace works on the principle that each star has a corresponding **physical association** in traditional Chinese medicine. By understanding which stars occupy this palace and how they are supported or challenged, you can identify potential health vulnerabilities and take proactive steps toward wellness.

This palace also reveals your **recovery ability** and **constitutional strength** -- how your body responds to illness, stress, and the demands of life.

**Note:** This palace is for self-awareness only. Always consult qualified medical professionals for health concerns.

## Key Stars in the Health Palace

## Key Stars and Health

### Stars and Their Health Associations:

**Zi Wei (紫微) -- Earth:**
Head, brain, nervous system, stomach. Worry and authority stress particularly affect this star's health domain.

**Tian Ji (天机) -- Wood:**
Liver, tendons, gall bladder. Anxiety and overthinking are the primary health vulnerabilities.

**Tai Yang (太阳) -- Fire:**
Eyes, heart, circulatory system. Excess heat, anger, and overexertion affect health.

**Wu Qu (武曲) -- Metal:**
Lungs, bones, respiratory system. Grief, loss, and respiratory illness are common concerns.

**Lian Zhen (廉贞) -- Fire:**
Sexual organs, blood, inflammation. Emotional turmoil frequently manifests as physical symptoms.

**Tian Tong (天同) -- Water:**
Kidneys, digestive system, bladder. Overindulgence and laziness are the primary health risks.

**Tian Yong (天梁) -- Metal:**
Skin, aging-related conditions, digestive issues. Guardian energy but also protective healing.

**Ji Men (巨门) -- Water:**
Stomach, digestive tract, throat, thyroid. Hidden worries and suppressed emotions manifest physically.

**Lu Cun (禄存) -- Wood:**
Liver, gall bladder. Financial stress and overwork frequently show as physical symptoms.

**Tao Hua (桃花) -- Water:**
Reproductive system, heart, emotional health. Romantic stress and overindulgence are the risks.

### Transformation Stars and Health:
- **Lu (Fortune)**: Generally protective, good recovery from illness
- **Jie (忌)**: Creates significant health vulnerabilities in the relevant body system

## How to Interpret Your Health Palace

## Interpreting Your Health Palace

### Step 1: Identify the Main Star
The main star in your Health Palace determines your **constitutional type**:
- **Strong positive stars**: Generally robust constitution
- **Challenging stars**: More vulnerable constitution requiring preventive care
- **Empty palace**: Generally healthy but without specific protection

### Step 2: Check the Transformations
Transformation stars modify health outcomes:
- **Lu nearby**: Recovery support, health luck, resilience
- **Jie (忌) nearby**: Chronic vulnerability, requires careful management

### Step 3: Look at Supporting Palaces
The Health Palace's adjacent palaces (三房) provide context:
- Strong support palaces = better health prognosis
- Weak support palaces = need more proactive health management

### Step 4: Cross-Reference with Age
Different life periods bring different health challenges:
- The Health Palace's condition at different life cycles matters
- Major transits can trigger dormant health patterns
- Health issues may manifest at specific age ranges

## Traditional Wellness Recommendations

Based on your Health Palace's star patterns:

### For Wood-related vulnerabilities (Tian Ji, Lu Cun):
- Liver-cleansing foods: green vegetables, sour foods in moderation
- Regular exercise to move liver Qi
- Manage anger and frustration constructively
- Adequate sleep before midnight
- Green tea in moderation

### For Fire-related vulnerabilities (Tai Yang, Lian Zhen):
- Cooling foods: bitter foods, watermelon, cucumber
- Stress management techniques (meditation, breathing)
- Cardiovascular exercise
- Anger management practices
- Avoid excess caffeine and spicy foods

### For Water-related vulnerabilities (Tian Tong, Tian Kong, Tao Hua):
- Kidney-supporting foods: black beans, walnuts, bone broth
- Adequate hydration with room-temperature water
- Warm foods and drinks
- Moderate lifestyle, avoid extremes
- Sufficient rest and sleep

### General Preventive Measures:
- Annual comprehensive health check-ups
- Pay attention to early warning signs
- Maintain emotional health through relationships and outlets
- Exercise appropriate to constitution type
- Don't ignore persistent symptoms
